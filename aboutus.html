About Us

The Bulls Head is on the A514 at Hartshorne, and it is a pub full of history and character.

It's imposing Georgian front was built in the early 18th century and the ground floor to the rear is thought to date back to Elizabethan times, around 1570 - 1600. The interior has a cosy welcoming atmosphere, with timber beams and a high wooden plinth adorned with memorabilia.

Hartshorne village church was rebuilt in 1835, though it retains its 15th century tower and 14th century font. A fine alter tomb shows the alabaster figures of Sir Humhrey Dethick of 1599 and his wife.

The Dethick's paid long and loyal service to the Royal family of their day - one of the Dethick's went to Cleves to find a fourth wife Henry VIII while his son Sir William is said to have laid a pall of rich velvet on the coffin of Mary, Queen of Scots.

We are a husband and wife team with some great staff in support. We do our best to give a really warm welcome to everyone who walks through the door.
